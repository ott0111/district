Brand Guidelines

Our brand identity.

Logo, colors, typography, and usage guidelines for the District brand.

Logo

Logo usage.

The District logo consists of the wordmark and icon. They can be used together or separately depending on the context. Always use the official logo files and do not modify the design.

  • Use the full logo (wordmark + icon) for primary brand representation
  • Use the icon-only version for compact spaces and app icons
  • Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "D" in District
  • Do not stretch, distort, or modify the logo proportions
  • Use the dark version on light backgrounds, light version on dark backgrounds
Colors

Color palette.

Primary Accent: #4F8CFF (Blue)

Background Primary: #050505 (Dark)

Background Secondary: #0B0B0B (Dark)

Background Card: #111111 (Dark)

Text Primary: #FFFFFF (White)

Text Secondary: #B3B3B3 (Light Gray)

Text Muted: #666666 (Gray)

Border: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.08)


Use these colors consistently across all brand materials. The dark background with white text creates our signature premium feel.

Typography

Typography.

Display Font: Outfit

Used for headings, titles, and display text. Weights: 400, 500, 600, 700


Body Font: Inter

Used for body text, UI elements, and interface copy. Weights: 400, 500, 600


Use Outfit for all headings and display elements to maintain brand consistency. Use Inter for body text and UI elements for optimal readability.

Usage

Usage guidelines.

Do:

  • Use the logo on dark backgrounds for optimal contrast
  • Maintain proper clear space around the logo
  • Use brand colors consistently
  • Apply typography hierarchy appropriately
  • Keep designs minimal and premium

Don't:

  • Don't modify the logo colors or proportions
  • Don't use the logo on busy or conflicting backgrounds
  • Don't add effects like shadows or gradients to the logo
  • Don't use unapproved fonts or colors
  • Don't stretch or distort brand assets
